## Vowel pair

Select which pair of vowels to analyze for overlap. Pairs are grouped by phonological environment.

### Prelateral

Vowels before /l/ are conditioned strongly by the following lateral — often more so than their elsewhere counterparts. Classic pairs include **feel–fill** (ZEAL vs. GUILT) and **fail–fell** (FLAIL vs. SHELF).

### Prenasal

Nasals raise and sometimes merge nearby vowels. **Pin–pen** (BIN vs. BEN) is one of the most studied mergers in American English, particularly in the South.

### Prevelar

The "bag–beg–back" complex involves raising and tensing of /æ/ before velars in many American dialects. Multiple pairs are provided to isolate different aspects of the shift.

### Other

- **Cot–caught** (BOT vs. BOUGHT) — the low-back merger, widespread across North America.
- **Goose-fronting** (TOOT vs. BOOT) — measures the degree of fronting in /u/ across different word-initial consonant contexts.
